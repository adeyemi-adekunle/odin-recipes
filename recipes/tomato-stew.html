Tomato Stew Recipes

Description

Nigerian tomato stew has a lovely red hue and delicious bold flavors.With rich tomatoes as its flavor base, this stew is truly enticing.

Additional flavor agents include onions, bouillon, curry, thyme, garlic, and peppers. What gives its distinct flavor, though, is the gamey ram. Because it's very expensive, this protein is considered celebratory meat and is often only served during special occasions, such as the Islamic holiday, Eid-el Kabir.

Ingredients

Meat

  • 2 lbs Braised ram meat chunks
  • 1 3/4 cups Lamb stock

Produce

  • 2 Bell peppers, large
  • 3 cloves Garlic
  • 1 Ginger
  • 2 Onions, large
  • 3 1/2 lbs Plum tomatoes
  • 4 Scotch bonnet peppers
  • 2 tsp Thyme, Dried

Canned Goods

  • 1 tsp Bouillon

Baking & Spices

  • 1 tsp Curry powder
  • 1 Salt

Oils and Vinegars

  • 1/2 cup Flavorless oil

Steps or Directions

  1. Blend the tomato mixture
    2. Blend the tomatoes, a single large onion, bell peppers, scotch bonnet peppers, garlic and ginger, and set aside.
  2. Broil and braise the meat
    3. Broil the braised lamb chunks in the oven until it is browned on both sides then set aside (check out this link on how to quickly and properly braise the meat or review the quick overview in the recipe notes below this recipe.
  3. Prep the tomato paste
    4. In a large pot Cook the blended tomato mixture on medium heat until it forms a paste. It should reduce to about half its volume. While the blended tomato mixture is reducing, slice one large onion and set aside.
  4. Caramelize the onions and add tomato paste
    5. Once the tomato mixture has reduced, scoop the paste out of the pot and set it aside. In the same pot, heat the oil on medium heat and caramelize the onions until it is mostly softened. Add the tomato paste to the caramelized onions and add the cook on low- medium heat for 5 minutes.
  5. Add meat stock and spices
    6. After 5 minutes, pour in the stock, and season with bouillon, dried thyme and curry powder. Stir and continue to cook for another 10 minutes on low heat.
  6. Add meat of choice
    7. After 10 minutes, add in the cooked ram, stir, and cook the stew covered for another 5 minutes on low heat. Taste for salt and adjust to your preference, and cook for another 5 minutes covered. After 5 minutes, your stew is ready to serve!
